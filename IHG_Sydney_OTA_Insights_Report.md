# IHG Sydney – OTA Sponsored Ad Performance
## Insights Report: Impact of Pausing Expedia & Booking.com Ads (Feb–Mar 2026)

---

## Executive Summary

Sponsored ads on Expedia and Booking.com were paused from February 2026. The purpose of this report is to assess whether this pause has had any material impact on OTA-sourced accommodation revenue and whether the always-on budget line should be removed in favour of a discretionary budget for need dates only.

**Bottom line:** There is no evidence that pausing sponsored OTA ads has negatively impacted Expedia or Booking.com revenue. The data supports moving to a discretionary-only model.

---

## 1. What the Sponsored Ads Actually Delivered (When Active)

When sponsored ads were running (May–Dec 2025), the ad platforms self-reported the following:

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Total ad spend (May–Dec 2025) | $13,210 AUD |
| Total ad-attributed revenue | $487,563 AUD |
| Average monthly spend | ~$1,650 AUD |
| Average monthly ad-attributed revenue | ~$60,945 AUD |
| Platform-reported ROAS | 36.9x |
| Total ad-attributed bookings | 222 (avg. 28/month) |

**On the surface, the ROAS looks strong at 36.9x.** However, this needs context.

### The Attribution Gap

During those same months (May–Dec 2025), the CRM recorded **$10,796,261 in total Expedia + Booking.com revenue** from arrivals. The sponsored ads claimed credit for just **$487,563 – or 4.5% of total platform revenue.**

- The ads attributed an average of **28 bookings/month** against a CRM average of **1,070 bookings/month** from these platforms
- Ad-attributed bookings represented **2–5% of total Expedia + Booking.com volume** in any given month
- The vast majority of OTA revenue is organic – driven by the property's listing, reviews, pricing, and availability

**Key insight:** The sponsored ads are a visibility boost on platforms where the property already generates substantial organic demand. The question is whether removing that boost causes any measurable decline.

---

## 2. The Pause Period – Did Revenue Decline?

### February & March: Year-on-Year Comparison

**Important context:** February and March 2025 also had minimal or zero OTA ad spend ($29 in Feb 2025, $0 in Mar 2025). This means the YoY comparison is effectively a like-for-like baseline – no ads in either year.

| Metric | Feb 2025 | Feb 2026 | YoY Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Expedia + BDC Revenue | $1,078,940 | $1,203,919 | **+$124,979 (+11.6%)** |
| Expedia + BDC Bookings | 810 | 624 | -186 (-23.0%) |
| Expedia + BDC Nights | 1,834 | 1,533 | -301 (-16.4%) |

| Metric | Mar 2025 | Mar 2026 | YoY Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Expedia + BDC Revenue | $1,047,425 | $1,365,756 | **+$318,331 (+30.4%)** |
| Expedia + BDC Bookings | 839 | 949 | +110 (+13.1%) |
| Expedia + BDC Nights | 1,959 | 2,275 | +316 (+16.1%) |

**Revenue grew YoY in both months despite zero sponsored ad spend.**

### February 2026 – Fewer Bookings, Higher Revenue

February shows an interesting split: revenue up but bookings down 23%. This points to higher average daily rates (ADR) driven by strong occupancy and demand-based pricing. External factors – including global uncertainty and some travel hesitancy – likely contributed to fewer individual bookings, but those that did book were at higher rate points. The property was well-occupied, and rates reflected that.

### March 2026 – Growth Across the Board

March is the cleaner read: revenue up 30.4%, bookings up 13.1%, nights up 16.1%. Across every metric, the platform performed better than the prior year with no sponsored ad support.

## 3. January Baseline – Ads Active Both Years

January is the only recent month where sponsored ads were active in both 2025 and 2026, providing a controlled comparison.

| Metric | Jan 2025 | Jan 2026 | YoY Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ad Spend | $1,785 | $1,785 | $0 |
| Ad-Attributed Revenue | $103,946 | $103,946 | $0 |
| CRM Expedia + BDC Revenue | $1,298,378 | $1,467,684 | +$169,306 (+13.0%) |
| CRM Expedia + BDC Bookings | 1,091 | 1,285 | +194 (+17.8%) |

Even with identical ad spend, the ad-attributed revenue represented just **7.1% of total CRM platform revenue**. The organic base is doing the heavy lifting.

---

## 4. Wider Channel Mix – No Displacement Effect

A common concern with pausing OTA ads is that bookings might shift to other channels or disappear entirely. The CRM data shows no displacement:

- **Total property revenue grew YoY** in both Feb (+25.1%) and Mar (+8.9%)
- **Expedia + Booking.com's share of total revenue** remained consistent: 13.2% in Feb 2026 (vs 14.8% Feb 2025) and 15.9% in Mar 2026 (vs 13.2% Mar 2025)
- **Direct channels (IHG Internet, App, Mobile Web) remained stable** – no meaningful shift from OTA to direct or vice versa
- Other OTA platforms (Agoda, Ctrip) also continued performing organically

The property's channel mix is healthy and not dependent on sponsored OTA ad spend.

## 5. Budget Impact

| Item | Amount |
|---|---|
| 2026 budgeted OTA line item (full year) | $9,860 AUD |
| Actual spend to date (Jan only) | $1,785 AUD |
| Remaining budgeted (if resumed Apr–Dec) | ~$8,075 AUD |

The annual OTA ad budget is a relatively small line item. However, based on the evidence, it is not generating incremental revenue above what the organic listings already deliver.

---

## Recommendation: Move to Discretionary Budget Only

Based on the data, the recommendation is to **remove the always-on OTA sponsored ad line item** and retain a discretionary budget to activate for specific need dates.

### Supporting Evidence

- **No revenue decline when ads were paused** – Expedia + Booking.com revenue grew YoY in both Feb (+11.6%) and Mar (+30.4%) 2026 with zero ad spend
- **Ads capture a small fraction of organic demand** – When active, sponsored ads attributed just 3–8% of total platform revenue and 2–5% of bookings
- **Self-reported ROAS is likely inflated** – OTA ad platforms attribute bookings that would likely have occurred organically through the listing. We cannot see attribution on other channels, but we can definitively see that the OTA platforms continued to deliver without paid support
- **Feb and Mar 2025 were also low/zero spend months** – The property performed well in those months too, reinforcing that organic listings drive the bulk of OTA revenue regardless of ad spend
- **Channel mix remained stable** – No evidence of demand loss or channel displacement

### Proposed Approach

- **Remove the always-on Expedia and Booking.com ad budget line items**
- **Retain a discretionary budget** (suggest ~$2,000–3,000 AUD per activation) to deploy tactically for:
  - Specific need dates where occupancy is below target
  - Seasonal soft periods identified by revenue management
  - Event-driven demand capture where incremental visibility may help
- **Monitor CRM Expedia + Booking.com revenue monthly** to confirm no degradation over time
- **Reallocate the freed budget (~$8,000–10,000 AUD annually)** to channels where attribution and incrementality can be more clearly demonstrated

---

### Important Caveat

We cannot see attribution data on channels outside the OTA ad platforms. The OTA platforms do report strong ROAS within their own ecosystems. However, the CRM data – which captures all bookings regardless of source – shows no material decline when ads are removed. The weight of evidence supports the organic listings as the primary revenue driver, with sponsored ads providing marginal (and likely non-incremental) visibility.
