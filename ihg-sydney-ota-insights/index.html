February 2026 – fewer bookings, higher revenue

Revenue grew +11.6% YoY but bookings declined -23%. This indicates higher ADR driven by strong occupancy and demand-based pricing. External factors – including global uncertainty and travel hesitancy – likely contributed to fewer individual bookings, but those that did book were at higher rate points. The property was well-occupied and rates reflected that demand.

March 2026 – growth across the board

March is the cleaner read: revenue up +30.4%, bookings up +13.1%, nights up +16.1%. Every metric improved year-on-year with zero sponsored ad support. This is the strongest evidence that the organic listing – not paid visibility – drives OTA performance.

The Attribution Question

What we can and can't see

An honest assessment of the data limitations and what the evidence tells us.

What we can see

  • OTA ad platforms report their own attributed revenue and ROAS
  • CRM captures all Expedia and Booking.com bookings (organic + ad-driven, mixed)
  • Total platform revenue before and after the pause
  • Year-on-year performance trends across all channels

What we can't see

  • Which specific CRM bookings were influenced by sponsored ads vs organic
  • Attribution on other channels (search, social, direct)
  • Whether any OTA bookings displaced to direct channels when ads paused
  • Halo effect of OTA visibility on brand awareness

The weight of evidence

We cannot definitively prove that sponsored ads cannibalise organic OTA demand. However, the CRM data – which captures all bookings regardless of source – shows no decline when ads are removed. The ad platforms self-report strong ROAS, but they only ever claimed 3–8% of total platform revenue. The remaining 92–97% came through organic listings with or without paid support.

The property's channel mix remained stable through the pause period. Total property revenue grew +25.1% in February and +8.9% in March YoY. Expedia + Booking.com's share of total revenue held consistent at 13–16%. There is no evidence of demand loss or channel displacement.

Recommendation

Remove always-on, retain discretionary budget

Based on the evidence, we recommend removing the always-on OTA sponsored ad line items and retaining a discretionary budget to activate for specific need dates only.

Recommendation

Remove always-on OTA line items

No evidence that the ~$1,650/month always-on spend generates incremental revenue above what organic listings deliver. Revenue grew YoY without it.

Savings: ~$8,000–$10,000/year
Retain

Keep discretionary budget

Retain a small discretionary pot (~$2,000–$3,000 per activation) to deploy tactically for specific need dates where occupancy is below target.

Use for: Need dates, soft periods, event capture
Action

Reallocate freed budget

Redirect the ~$8,000–$10,000 annual savings to channels where attribution and incrementality can be more clearly demonstrated.

Channels: Paid search, paid social, audience growth
Ongoing monitoring: We recommend tracking Expedia + Booking.com CRM revenue monthly to confirm no degradation over time. If a sustained decline emerges that cannot be attributed to external factors, the discretionary budget provides the flexibility to re-activate quickly.

Supporting evidence summary

  • No revenue decline when ads were paused – Expedia + Booking.com revenue grew +11.6% (Feb) and +30.4% (Mar) YoY with zero ad spend
  • Ads captured a small fraction of organic demand – When active, sponsored ads attributed just 3–8% of total platform revenue and 2–5% of bookings
  • Self-reported ROAS is likely inflated – OTA ad platforms attribute bookings that would likely have occurred organically through the listing
  • Feb & Mar 2025 were also low/zero spend months – The property performed well in those months too, reinforcing that organic listings drive the bulk of OTA revenue
  • Channel mix remained stable – No evidence of demand loss or channel displacement during the pause period
