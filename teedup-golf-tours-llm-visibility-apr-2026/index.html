5 things the board should know

1. When people ask AI about golf tours, Tee'd Up is almost never mentioned

We tested 120 questions across ChatGPT and Gemini. When users ask about "golf tours from Australia" or "best escorted golf tours" without naming Tee'd Up, the brand appears in only 11% of responses. Competitors like Go Golfing, The Travelling Golfer, and PerryGolf appear in almost every response.

2. AI is already sending you business - and it recommended us to you

Bede found Webprofits by asking Claude for recommendations - living proof that AI is how your customers research high-value purchases. Tee'd Up has received 8 website visits from AI platforms (ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity) in the last 2.5 months. This number will grow significantly. Being absent from AI responses means being absent from your customers' consideration set.

3. Your biggest selling point is invisible to AI

Having a PGA professional personally host every tour is Tee'd Up's strongest differentiator. But AI doesn't know this. When asked "best hosted golf tours where a pro plays with you," competitors are named instead. The website doesn't make this story findable enough for AI systems.

4. Gemini is your friend - but only when people name you

Google's Gemini AI can find Tee'd Up for branded queries (100% success) because it uses Google Search data. ChatGPT struggles even with branded queries (41%). But neither platform recommends Tee'd Up when users search by category - which is how most new customers discover tour operators.

5. You have hundreds of testimonials and 5-star Google Reviews - but AI can't find them

Tee'd Up has hundreds of testimonials and fantastic Google 5-star reviews from 3,000+ past guests across a 12,000-person database. But the new website only surfaces 3 testimonials. AI systems can't access or cite what isn't visible on the web. Surfacing this existing social proof - not collecting new reviews - is the fastest path to improving AI credibility signals.

Why this matters now

AI-powered search is growing rapidly. Your target demographic - retired Australians aged 50+ - are increasingly using ChatGPT and Gemini to research travel options. Every day that Tee'd Up is absent from AI responses is a day your competitors are being recommended instead. The brands that invest in AI visibility now will own the narrative for years to come, because today's content becomes tomorrow's AI training data.

Strategic roadmap

Prioritised actions to improve AI visibility

Priority 1 - Immediate (credibility and content foundations)

1

Fix critical page speed issues

Optimise images (convert to WebP, lazy-load below-fold), eliminate render-blocking resources, remove unused JS/CSS. Target: LCP under 4 seconds. AI crawlers need fast, reliable pages to index content.

2

Add meta descriptions to every page

The homepage has no meta description. AI uses meta descriptions as a primary signal for understanding page content. Write unique, keyword-rich descriptions for every tour page, category page, and blog post.

3

Build Google Business Profile with 20+ reviews

Gemini pulls GBP data directly. Email past guests asking for Google Reviews. Target: 20+ reviews within 4 weeks. This single action will have the highest impact on Gemini's reputation responses.

4

Add TripAdvisor and ProductReview.com.au profiles

Both ChatGPT and Gemini direct users to these platforms when asked about reviews. Currently, they find nothing. Claim profiles and solicit reviews from past guests.

5

Fix HTTPS and heading hierarchy

Resolve the insecure request (mixed content), fix heading order (skipped levels), and ensure all links have discernible names. Quick technical fixes that improve both accessibility and AI crawlability.

Priority 2 - 1-3 months (technical + content)

6

Implement structured data across all pages

Add TravelAgency, Product/Offer (for each tour), Event (tour dates), FAQPage, Review/AggregateRating, and Person schema. This makes tour details machine-readable for AI extraction.

Create 15-20 category-defining content pieces

Write authoritative articles that directly answer the questions AI users ask. Each piece should be 1,500-2,500 words with expert quotes from Mike Mosher.

8

Build out the "non-golfer partner" content hub

This is uncontested territory in AI responses. Create a dedicated page/section about what non-playing partners experience on each tour, with specific activities, testimonials from partners, and photo galleries. This positions Tee'd Up as the authority on couples golf travel.

9

Add author bylines and Mike Mosher expert commentary

Every blog post and destination guide should have a named author with credentials. Mike Mosher's PGA professional status should be front-and-centre on all content - this is an E-E-A-T signal AI systems weight heavily.

Surface existing testimonials (25+ minimum)

Tee'd Up has hundreds of testimonials from 3,000+ past guests - but the new website only displays 3. Upload at least 25-50 of the best, with full names, tour details, photos, and specific experiences. Add Review schema markup to each. List on industry directories and associations

Develop comparison content

Publish "How Tee'd Up differs" and "Why choose a hosted golf tour" content that AI can use to form comparative assessments. Currently, AI positions Tee'd Up as "smaller/boutique" because there's no content asserting a stronger positioning.

Investigate Wikipedia notability

With 25+ years of operation, Tee'd Up may meet Wikipedia notability criteria. A Wikipedia stub (if achievable) dramatically boosts AI entity recognition. This requires genuine third-party coverage to justify inclusion.
